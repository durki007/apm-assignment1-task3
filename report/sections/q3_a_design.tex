\subsection{(a) Named entities and process behavior}
\label{sec:a}

\paragraph{Object types.}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering\small
\caption{Object types and their meanings.}
\label{tab:object-types}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}lX@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Object type} & \textbf{Meaning} \\
\midrule
\otPassenger  & An individual traveller (athlete, official, or spectator) who participates in the journey. \\
\otTicket     & A travel ticket issued to exactly one passenger; carries the static attribute \attrFare. \\
\otBooking    & A group reservation covering one or more passengers (a delegation or fan group). \\
\otDeparture  & A scheduled shuttle or train run that departs with a fixed, pre-assigned batch of passengers. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\paragraph{Event types.}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering\small
\caption{Event types and their meanings.}
\label{tab:event-types}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}lX@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Event type} & \textbf{Meaning} \\
\midrule
\etBooking   & A group reservation is opened for a set of passengers belonging to the same delegation or group. \\
\etIssue     & A travel ticket is issued to a single passenger, binding the passenger to their fare class. \\
\etAssign    & A departure is assigned a batch of passengers; records the complete set that will board together. \\
\etBoard     & An accredited passenger (fare class \texttt{accredited}) boards via the dedicated accreditation lane. \\
\etValidate  & A standard passenger (fare class \texttt{standard}) validates their ticket at the public gate. \\
\etDepart    & The departure vehicle leaves with its assigned passenger batch; synchronizes all batch members. \\
\etComplete  & A passenger has arrived at their destination and completed the journey. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\paragraph{Process behavior.}
The process begins when a group coordinator opens a \otBooking, enrolling a set
of \otPassenger{} objects into a shared reservation via \etBooking.
Each passenger then receives a \otTicket{} through \etIssue, with
\attrFare{} set statically at issuance to either \texttt{accredited} or
\texttt{standard}.
Before a departure, all passengers assigned to a given \otDeparture{} are
recorded together in a single \etAssign{} event, establishing the batch that
will travel together.
Passengers then proceed to boarding: accredited passengers predominantly use
\etBoard{} while standard passengers predominantly use \etValidate{}, with the
choice driven by their \attrFare{} value.
Finally, a single \etDepart{} event fires for the entire batch simultaneously,
after which each passenger records their arrival individually via \etComplete.
