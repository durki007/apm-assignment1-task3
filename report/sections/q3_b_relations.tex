\subsection{(b) Type-level E2O and O2O relations}
\label{sec:b}

The tables below are derived directly from the model definition in
\texttt{config.py} via \texttt{render\_tables.py}, ensuring consistency with
the generated log.

\paragraph{Event-to-object (E2O) relations.}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering\small
\caption{E2O type-level relations. Each row lists the object types touched by
the event type and the semantic role of that relationship.
Satisfies R(iv): every event type touches at least one object type and every
object type is touched by at least one event type.}
\label{tab:e2o}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}llX@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Event type} & \textbf{Object type} & \textbf{Relation description} \\
\midrule
\etBooking   & \otPassenger & Records all passengers enrolled in the group reservation. \\
             & \otBooking   & Records the group reservation object that is opened. \\
\addlinespace
\etIssue     & \otPassenger & Records the passenger to whom the ticket is issued. \\
             & \otTicket    & Records the ticket that is created and assigned. \\
\addlinespace
\etAssign    & \otPassenger & Records all passengers assigned to the departure batch. \\
             & \otDeparture & Records the departure to which the batch is assigned. \\
\addlinespace
\etBoard     & \otPassenger & Records the accredited passenger boarding via the fast lane. \\
             & \otTicket    & Records the ticket scanned at the accreditation gate. \\
\addlinespace
\etValidate  & \otPassenger & Records the standard-fare passenger validating at the public gate. \\
             & \otTicket    & Records the ticket validated at the public gate. \\
\addlinespace
\etDepart    & \otPassenger & Records all passengers on board when the vehicle departs. \\
             & \otDeparture & Records the departure vehicle that leaves. \\
\addlinespace
\etComplete  & \otPassenger & Records the passenger who has completed the journey. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}
