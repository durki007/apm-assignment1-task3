\subsection{(g) Custom business query}
\label{sec:g}

\textbf{Business question.}
\emph{Which \etDepart{} events carry more than $N = 2$ standard-fare passengers?}

\textbf{Motivation.}
Standard-fare passengers validate at the public gate (\etValidate), which is a
shared resource with limited throughput; departures with many standard-fare
passengers risk queue build-up and missed departure windows.
Identifying these high-load departures allows dispatchers to pre-position
additional gate staff or open extra lanes before the affected \etDepart{} fires,
directly supporting operational planning for the 2036 Olympic Games.
The query combines E2O relations (linking \etDepart{} to \otPassenger) with the
O2O \texttt{holds} relation and the \attrFare{} attribute of \otTicket, making
it a genuinely object-centric query that cannot be answered from a flat event
log alone.
